# Scout Application - Primer

**Audience:** Non-technical, Product Managers  
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-26

## What is a Scout?

A Scout is a verified member of the Cofactor Scout community with:
- **Green "Verified Scout" badge** on submissions
- **Priority review** of research leads
- **Higher commission rates** on successful matches
- **Part of official Scout network**

## Contributor vs Scout

### Contributor (Default Role)
- Automatically assigned on sign-up
- Can submit research leads immediately
- Standard commission rates
- Gray badge on submissions

### Scout (Verified Role)
- Must apply and be approved
- Priority review of submissions
- Higher commission rates
- Green "Verified Scout" badge
- Part of official network

## Application Process

### Step 1: Fill Out Application

**Required Information:**
- University affiliation
- Department
- Your role (PhD student, postdoc, professor, etc.)
- Research areas of expertise
- Why you want to be a Scout
- How you'll source research leads

**Optional:**
- LinkedIn profile

### Step 2: Submit Application

- Application is reviewed by Cofactor team
- Status changes to "Pending"
- You receive confirmation email

### Step 3: Team Review

**What we look for:**
- Strong university affiliation
- Relevant research background
- Clear understanding of what makes research investment-worthy
- Realistic plan for sourcing leads
- Professional communication

**Timeline:** 1-2 weeks

### Step 4: Decision

**If Approved:**
- Role upgraded to SCOUT
- Badge changes to green "Verified Scout"
- Higher commission rates apply
- Welcome email with next steps

**If Rejected:**
- Can reapply after 30 days
- Feedback provided (if requested)
- Can continue as Contributor

## Application While Signed In

If you already have an account:
1. Go to Dashboard
2. Click "Apply to be a Scout"
3. Fill out application form
4. Submit
5. Wait for review

Your existing submissions and drafts remain unchanged.

## Application While Signing Up

If you don't have an account yet:
1. Go to Scout Application page
2. Fill out application AND create account
3. Verify your email
4. Application automatically submitted
5. Wait for review

This creates your account and submits your Scout application in one step.

## After Approval

### What Changes
- Badge on all submissions (past and future)
- Higher commission on new matches
- Priority review queue
- Access to Scout resources (future)

### What Stays the Same
- Your existing submissions
- Your drafts
- Your account settings
- Your submission workflow

## Common Questions

**Q: Can I submit research leads while my application is pending?**  
A: Yes! You can submit as a Contributor while waiting for approval.

**Q: What happens to my existing submissions if I'm approved?**  
A: They get the Scout badge and higher commission rates apply to future matches.

**Q: How long does review take?**  
A: Typically 1-2 weeks. We review applications in the order received.

**Q: What if I'm rejected?**  
A: You can reapply after 30 days. You can continue submitting as a Contributor.

**Q: Do I need to be affiliated with a university?**  
A: Yes, Scouts should have strong university connections to source quality research.

**Q: Can I apply if I'm an undergraduate?**  
A: Yes, but we prioritize PhD students, postdocs, and professors who have deeper research networks.

**Q: What makes a strong application?**  
A: Clear research expertise, realistic sourcing plan, professional communication, strong university affiliation.

**Q: Can I update my application after submitting?**  
A: No, but you can contact support@cofactor.world to add information.

**Q: What if my university isn't listed?**  
A: Type it in manually. We accept all universities worldwide.

## Tips for Strong Applications

1. **Be specific about research areas** — "Machine learning for drug discovery" is better than "AI"
2. **Explain your network** — How will you find research leads?
3. **Show understanding** — What makes research investment-worthy?
4. **Be professional** — This is a business relationship
5. **Be realistic** — Don't overpromise on lead volume

## What We're Looking For

**Strong indicators:**
- PhD student or higher
- Active in research community
- Attends conferences
- Reads academic papers regularly
- Has lab/department connections
- Understands commercialization
- Clear communication

**Red flags:**
- No university affiliation
- Vague research areas
- Unrealistic sourcing plans
- Poor communication
- No understanding of investment criteria
